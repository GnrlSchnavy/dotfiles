# Drift Detection — /vault-drift

You will compare what I said I would do against what I actually did. Surface the gap between intention and action.

## Vault Location
`/Users/yvan/Documents/Obsidian/Yvan_claude`

## Instructions

### Step 1: Collect stated intentions
Read daily notes, project files, and task lists from the last 30-60 days. Extract:
- Explicit goals and plans ("I want to...", "I should...", "Plan is to...")
- Task lists (especially unchecked items that persist)
- Project descriptions and stated priorities
- Commitments made in meeting notes or journal entries

### Step 2: Collect actual behavior
From the same period, map what actually happened:
- Which notes got written and updated?
- Which topics show up repeatedly in daily notes?
- Which tasks actually got completed?
- What new notes/projects appeared that weren't planned?
- Where did time and attention actually go?

### Step 3: Find the drift
Compare intentions vs actions:
- **Stated priorities I'm ignoring** — things I said matter but rarely show up in actual activity
- **Unplanned attention magnets** — topics getting significant time that I never planned for
- **Persistent avoidance** — tasks or projects that appear repeatedly but never get done
- **Silent progress** — things advancing without being explicitly planned
- **Abandoned without closure** — projects or goals that simply stopped being mentioned

### Step 4: Present the drift analysis
Structure as:

**Intention vs Reality Map** — Side-by-side of stated priorities vs actual attention
**What you're avoiding** — The clearest cases of stated-but-not-done
**What's pulling you** — Unplanned topics getting real attention
**Persistent open loops** — Tasks that keep appearing but never close
**The honest priority list** — What your behavior suggests your actual priorities are (vs what you say they are)
**One question** — A direct question about the biggest gap
