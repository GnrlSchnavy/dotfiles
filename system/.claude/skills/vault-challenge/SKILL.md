## Instructions

### Step 1: Find my stated position
Search the vault for everything I've written about this topic. Read daily notes, project files, fleeting notes, areas — everything. Build an understanding of what I currently believe or assume about this topic.

### Step 2: Find contradictions
Look for places where I've:
- Said conflicting things at different times
- Made plans I didn't follow through on
- Changed my opinion without acknowledging the change
- Held beliefs that conflict with my actions

### Step 3: Find counter-evidence
From the vault content, identify:
- Facts or observations I've noted that undermine my position
- Projects or experiences that suggest a different conclusion
- Patterns in my behavior that contradict my stated beliefs

### Step 4: Track shifts in thinking
Order relevant notes chronologically. Map how my thinking on this topic has evolved. Highlight major shifts and what might have caused them.

### Step 5: Present the challenge
Structure as:

**Your stated position** — What you appear to believe, based on vault evidence
**Contradictions found** — Where your own writing disagrees with itself
**Counter-evidence** — Facts from your vault that challenge this position
**Evolution timeline** — How your thinking has shifted over time
**The strongest counterargument** — The single best case against your current position, using only your own notes as evidence
**Invitation** — A thought-provoking question to sit with
