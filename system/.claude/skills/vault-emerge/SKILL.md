# Emerge — /vault-emerge

You will surface ideas that my vault implies but I've never explicitly stated. Look for conclusions hiding in scattered premises, patterns I haven't named, and directions my thinking is heading without me realizing it.

## Vault Location
`/Users/yvan/Documents/Obsidian/Yvan_claude`

## Instructions

### Step 1: Deep vault scan
Read broadly across the vault — MOCs, projects, areas, daily notes, fleeting notes. Don't focus on any single domain. The goal is to see the full landscape.

### Step 2: Identify unnamed patterns
Look for:
- **Recurring themes** across different domains that I haven't connected (e.g., the same principle showing up in cooking, software, and personal notes)
- **Implicit conclusions** — where multiple notes, taken together, lead to a conclusion I've never written down
- **Unarticulated directions** — where my recent notes are heading toward something I haven't named yet
- **Latent interests** — topics I keep circling back to without making a dedicated note about
- **Hidden frameworks** — mental models I seem to use repeatedly but haven't made explicit

### Step 3: Check the gaps
Look at orphaned notes, dead-end MOCs, and areas with few connections. Sometimes the most interesting emergent ideas live in the gaps between well-developed areas.

If Obsidian CLI is available:
- `obsidian orphans` — notes with no incoming links
- `obsidian tags` — distribution of attention

### Step 4: Present emergent ideas
For each idea you surface, provide:

**The emergent idea** — Name it clearly (this might be the first time it's named)
**Evidence trail** — Which specific notes lead to this conclusion, with quotes
**Why it matters** — What this means for my projects, thinking, or direction
**What to do with it** — Should this become a note? A project? Just something to sit with?

## Rules

- These should be genuine insights, not obvious observations
- You're looking for things I would say "oh, I never noticed that" about
- Be specific — reference actual notes and content, not vague patterns
